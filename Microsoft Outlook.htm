Master Outlook: 66 Keyboard Shortcuts You Can't Miss

You'll navigate your email inbox and outbox effortlessly like an expert once you master these Microsoft Outlook hotkeys for Windows.

If you rely on Outlook to manage your emails, schedule meetings, or delegate tasks, you've probably wished for more efficient ways to handle these administrative activities. Luckily, Outlook shortcuts can help you save time by performing common tasks more quickly. Instead of using a mouse to scroll, point, and click, you can simply press one or more keys on your keyboard. While you're learning these, you'll also want to remember these Windows keyboard shortcuts.

Keyboard shortcuts not only enhance your productivity but can also reduce the risk of repetitive stress injuries caused by extensive mouse use. They are also beneficial for individuals with mobility or visual impairments. Additionally, knowing these Word shortcuts can be extremely useful.

To activate and utilize shortcuts

1. Navigate to "Settings" from your main screen.

2. Proceed to "Additional mail settings."

3. Access "Customizing Outlook" and choose "Keyboard Shortcuts."

4. Pick your preferred mode and then save your changes.

Understanding the common notation for shortcuts is also beneficial. Some involve the Ctrl key along with other keys. Simultaneously pressed keys are denoted by a plus sign (+), while sequentially pressed keys are separated by a comma (,). Presented below are some of the most useful keyboard shortcuts in Outlook.

Personalizing Shortcuts:

To customize the keys assigned to existing actions, you can allocate new shortcuts within Outlook's settings. Begin by accessing the Categories section, then navigate to the "File Tab." From there, in the Commands section, opt for "File Open." Locate a command, and then select "Press new shortcut key." Input the desired key combination for that command. Click "Assign," and your customization is complete.
